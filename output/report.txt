Pandemic Impact Analysis Report
==================================================

Objective:
To analyze the impact of COVID-19 across countries using population-normalized metrics.

Top 5 Countries by Total Cases:
- United States: 103436829 cases
- China: 99373219 cases
- France: 38997490 cases
- Germany: 38437756 cases
- Brazil: 37511921 cases

Top 5 Countries by Cases per Million:
- Brunei: 774435.3 per million
- San Marino: 751396.32 per million
- Austria: 680392.01 per million
- South Korea: 667207.04 per million
- Faroe Islands: 652815.97 per million

Top 5 Countries by Death Rate:
- Yemen: 18.07%
- Sudan: 7.89%
- Syria: 5.51%
- Somalia: 4.98%
- Peru: 4.88%

Key Insights:
- Countries with large populations tend to report higher total cases.
- Smaller countries often show higher per capita infection rates.
- Death rate varies significantly, indicating differences in healthcare systems.
- Per capita metrics provide a more accurate comparison than total values.

Analysis Interpretation:
The analysis shows that raw case counts can be misleading when comparing countries.
Population-normalized metrics reveal the actual severity of the pandemic.
Some smaller nations experienced disproportionately high infection rates.

Conclusion:
Population size strongly influences total case counts, but per capita analysis provides a clearer understanding of pandemic impact.
